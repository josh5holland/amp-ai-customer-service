# Karting Classes & Age Eligibility

## Minimum Requirements
- **Minimum age:** 5 years old (no maximum)
- **Minors under 18:** Parent/guardian signature required, must be accompanied at facility

---

## Classes by Age Group

### Kid Kart (Ages 5-7)
- LO206 Kid Kart
- Comer Kid Kart

**Notes:** Smallest chassis, limited speed, perfect introduction for young children

### Cadet (Ages 7-12)
- LO206 Cadet
- TaG Rookie
- TaG Cadet

**Notes:** Transitional class, builds skills before moving to faster equipment

### Junior (Ages 12-15)
- LO206 Junior
- TaG Junior

**Notes:** More powerful than Cadet, preparing for Senior classes

### Senior (Ages 15+)
- LO206 Senior
- TaG Senior
- Shifter classes

**Notes:** Full-power adult classes, most competitive divisions

---

## Engine Types Explained

### Four-Stroke (LO206)
- **Maintenance:** Lower - pump gas, longer rebuild intervals
- **Power:** Moderate - great for learning racecraft
- **Cost:** Lower startup and ongoing costs
- **Best for:** BEGINNERS (recommended starting point)

**Why LO206 is recommended for beginners:**
- Pump gas instead of race fuel
- Less frequent maintenance
- Lower operating costs
- Emphasizes driver skill over equipment
- Active club racing community

### Two-Stroke (IAME, Rotax, TaG)
- **Maintenance:** Higher - race fuel, more frequent rebuilds
- **Power:** Higher - faster lap times
- **Cost:** Higher startup and ongoing costs
- **Best for:** Experienced racers

**Types:**
- **TaG (Touch-and-Go):** Electric start, moderate power
- **Shifter:** Sequential gearbox, highest power, most challenging

## Progression Pathway

```
Kid Kart (5-7)
    ↓
Cadet (7-12)
    ↓
Junior (12-15)
    ↓
Senior (15+)
    ↓
Shifter (experienced adults)
```

**Engine progression:**
```
LO206 (beginner) → TaG (intermediate) → Shifter (advanced)
```

---

## Class Selection Decision Tree

1. **What is the driver's age?**
   - 5-7 → Kid Kart
   - 7-12 → Cadet
   - 12-15 → Junior
   - 15+ → Senior

2. **What is their experience level?**
   - New to karting → LO206 engine class
   - Experienced karter → Consider TaG or Shifter

3. **What is their budget?**
   - Budget-conscious → LO206 (lower ongoing costs)
   - Performance-focused → TaG or Shifter
