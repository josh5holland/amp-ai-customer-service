# Escalation Criteria

## Immediate Escalation Required

### Safety Concerns
**Escalate when customer mentions:**
- Injuries (past or potential)
- Accidents or incidents
- Equipment failures
- Near-miss situations
- Unsafe conditions
- Medical emergencies

**Why:** Safety is paramount. Human judgment needed for documentation, investigation, and response.

### Legal/Liability Issues
**Escalate when customer mentions:**
- Lawyers or attorneys
- Intent to sue
- Insurance claims
- Liability questions
- Legal documentation requests

**Why:** Legal matters require proper handling and documentation.

### Financial Disputes
**Escalate when customer:**
- Requests refunds
- Claims overcharges
- Has billing disputes
- Questions charges
- Requests payment plans

**Why:** Financial decisions require authorization and account access.

### Negative Sentiment
**Escalate when customer displays:**
- Anger or frustration
- ALL CAPS communication
- Multiple exclamation marks
- Threat language
- Demand for manager/supervisor
- "Never again" / "warn others" language

**Why:** De-escalation and relationship recovery need human touch.

### Media/PR Situations
**Escalate when:**
- Media outlet contacts
- Reporter inquiries
- Influencer requests for partnership
- PR-sensitive situations
- Social media threats

**Why:** Public relations requires careful, authorized responses.

---

## Conditional Escalation

### Complex Requests
**Escalate when:**
- Corporate event planning (especially 20+ people)
- Custom package requests
- Multi-activity coordination
- Special accommodations needed
- Group arrangements

**Why:** Custom work requires human creativity and pricing authority.

### Out of Scope Questions
**Escalate when:**
- Technical vehicle advice needed
- Medical/fitness questions
- Policy exception requests
- Questions AI cannot confidently answer

**Why:** Accuracy matters; don't guess on important topics.

### Previous Issues
**Escalate when customer references:**
- Past complaints
- Ongoing unresolved issues
- Previous negative experiences
- Follow-up on earlier contact

**Why:** Context and history important for resolution.

---

## Do NOT Escalate

### Normal Inquiries
- General pricing questions
- Event/schedule questions
- Booking assistance
- Basic how-to questions
- Membership tier comparisons

### Simple Routing
- Directing to correct skill
- Providing booking links
- Sharing contact information
- General facility information

---

## Confidence-Based Escalation

If uncertain about an answer:
- **High uncertainty:** Escalate
- **Medium uncertainty:** Provide caveat, offer to escalate
- **Low uncertainty:** Answer with appropriate qualifier

"I'm not 100% certain about that. Would you like me to connect you with someone who can give you a definitive answer?"
